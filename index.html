Self-Driving Car using Conditional Imitation Learning

Award-Winning

I worked in a small team of four people with a non-profit Endeavr to create a self-driving car from scratch using conditional imitation learning.

What is Imitation learning? It is a form of machine learning where you train your model to try to imitate a human's behavior. We do this by first having an "Expert Driver" drive the car while we collect data like the inputs (dash cam video frame, speed of your car, and a turn-by-turn navigation instructions like turn left, right, or go straight) and the outputs, which is what our expert driver is doing (turning angle, gas, brake). After we collect all of this data, we train our model given each input to try to predict the turning angle, gas, and brake.

The main problem we encountered doing this is that the expert driver only gave it near-perfect data. This means our model does not know what to do if it makes a slight mistake. This is where we need to include DAgger. For this, we need to let the car drive; then, when it messes up, we take over the wheel while collecting data to get it back on track and let it take over. This data will be used to retrain the model to teach it to fix its mistakes.

The way that we approached this was we initially wanted to make sure that this was possible at all, so we made a simplified 2D version to test the waters. I found a random 2d racetrack image off Google, and our goal was to get a rectangle car to do laps around the track without falling off. I took a birds-eye view 'screenshot' of the car facing the same direction the car is facing to feed it into the model as the input image to give it context about its surroundings in the same way that we would with cameras on a 3D version. When we finished this initial prototype, it ran for hours without falling off the race track.

We eventually moved this model over to a 3D version to make our self-driving car work in CARLA, which is a driving simulator designed for creating autonomous cars. The car driving in the video is our self-driving car driving around when we were almost finished with the project. The final project can drive around like in the video with turn-by-turn navigation instructions (like turn left, turn right, or go straight) so that you can use something like Google Maps to give it inputs, and it will take you from point A to point B in your car to get you to your destination without human intervention.
